1. INTRODUCTION

Apna Doctor ("we," "our," "Company," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application ("Apna Doctor" or the "App").

This policy complies with applicable United States federal and state privacy laws including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), the California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA), the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) where applicable, the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA), the Colorado Privacy Act (CPA), the Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA), the Utah Consumer Privacy Act (UCPA), and Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 603A.

Please read this policy carefully. By using the App, you consent to the collection and use of your information as described herein.

3. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We use the information we collect for the following business purposes:

- To create, maintain, and manage your user account
- To facilitate appointment booking, confirmation, rescheduling, and cancellation
- To enable real-time text, audio, and video communication between patients and doctors
- To display doctor search results, profiles, and availability to patients
- To store, display, and share medical records between you and your treating physician
- To provide general health information through the AI Health Assistant feature
- To send push notifications regarding incoming calls, messages, and appointment reminders
- To maintain and improve the App's functionality and user experience
- To comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service

10. HIPAA COMPLIANCE NOTICE

Apna Doctor facilitates the exchange of Protected Health Information (PHI) between patients and healthcare providers.

- Medical data (diagnoses, treatment plans, prescriptions, medical records) is considered PHI.
- PHI is stored securely on Google Cloud Platform (Firebase Firestore and Firebase Storage).
- PHI is only accessible to the patient and their treating doctor.
- We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect PHI.
- We do not use PHI for marketing purposes.
- We obtain your authorization before using or disclosing PHI for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

Please note: Users should verify with their healthcare provider whether a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is in place for HIPAA-covered entities.

11. DO NOT TRACK (CalOPPA COMPLIANCE)

California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA) requires us to disclose how we respond to Do Not Track (DNT) signals.

We do not currently respond to DNT browser signals as no standard has been adopted. If a standard is established, we will update this policy accordingly.

Third parties do not collect personal information about your online activities over time across different websites or services through the App.

12. DATA STORAGE AND SECURITY

12.1 Storage Infrastructure
- Primary database: Google Cloud Firestore (Firebase)
- File storage: Firebase Storage
- Authentication: Firebase Authentication
- Local device storage: SharedPreferences (non-sensitive preferences only)

12.2 Security Measures
- All data transmitted between the App and servers is encrypted using TLS/HTTPS
- Passwords are hashed and managed via Firebase Authentication
- Firebase Security Rules restrict data access based on user authentication and ownership
- Access to medical records is restricted to the specific patient and their treating doctor
- Chat messages are restricted to the conversation participants
- We implement network security, access controls, and regular security assessments

12.3 Data Location
Data is processed and stored on Google Cloud servers located in the United States.
